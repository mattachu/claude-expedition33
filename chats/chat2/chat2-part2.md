**Maelle** (endgame DPS with Lithum)

The core loop is getting into and staying in Virtuose Stance, then nuking. Stendhal and Sword Ballet are her primary single-target damage tools in Virtuose, and Burning Canvas is a devastating fire nuke. Gustave's Homage benefits from marked targets, and Lithum's passive maintains marks rather than consuming them, making the two synergise well.

Recommended 6:
1. **Stendhal** — primary Virtuose nuke
2. **Burning Canvas** — fire burst (extremely powerful, confirmed as a key endgame skill)
3. **Sword Ballet** — crit-heavy backup burst
4. **Fleuret Fury** — breaking + multi-hit
5. **Last Chance** — re-enters Virtuose Stance when lost
6. **Payback** — high-damage counter after parries, good AP synergy

Swift Stride is a viable alternative to Last Chance if you prefer proactive stance entry. I'm less certain about the relative priority of Payback vs Gustave's Homage in slot 6 — both appear in endgame builds, and the choice may depend on how reliably you're landing parries.

---

**Verso** (endgame DPS with Chevalam)

With Chevalam in Act 3, you start at S Rank immediately, so you don't need to burn turns climbing to it — you can deal damage from turn one. That makes the rank-climbing skills (Quick Strike, Assault Zero) less critical, though still useful for Perfection maintenance after hits.

End Bringer is described as Verso's strongest skill — extreme damage for 6 hits, with bonus damage and extended stun at Rank A. Steeled Strike hits 13 times, making it excellent for multi-hit scaling when paired with Cheater.

Recommended 6 (playstyle-filtered — no Defiant Strike):
1. **Steeled Strike** — 13-hit primary damage skill
2. **End Bringer** — 6-hit nuke, bonus vs stunned enemies
3. **Quick Strike** — Perfection gain + fallback rank tool
4. **Assault Zero** — AP recovery + rank maintenance
5. **Marking Shot** — applies Mark safely, no HP cost
6. **Perfect Break** — single-target breaking + high damage

Phantom Stars could replace Perfect Break if you're fighting groups, but it's useless in 1v1. For boss fights, Perfect Break is cleaner.

Note: Verso currently doesn't have Steeled Strike or End Bringer — both require progression (Steeled Strike needs Cheater Pictos from Sprong first).

---

**Sciel** (pure support)

Fortune's Fury, Intervention, and Delaying Slash are cited as making Sciel by far the best support in the game — every top endgame setup starts with Sciel.

In Act 3, Fortune's Fury becomes her most impactful tool — doubling damage scales harder as numbers grow uncapped, and Intervention lets an ally act immediately with 4 AP, controlling turn order precisely.

Recommended 6:
1. **Fortune's Fury** — double an ally's damage (Maelle primary target)
2. **Intervention** — give ally an immediate turn + 4 AP
3. **Delaying Slash** — delays enemy's next turn (powerful boss control, confirmed as a standout skill)
4. **Plentiful Harvest** — AP generation to ally based on Foretell stacks
5. **Focused Foretell** — Foretell generation (enables Plentiful Harvest + Delaying Slash)
6. **Final Path** — her hardest-hitting damage skill when you have a free slot

The 5th and 6th slots are more flexible than 1–4. Sealed Fate or Twilight Dance could replace Final Path if you want more damage or Twilight extension respectively. Grim Harvest is an option if you need party healing, though your current Sciel build doesn't prioritise that role.

**Endgame party viability first:** Yes, Lune sees significant endgame use — she's described as an endgame nuker with explosive elemental burst, capable of turn looping with Cheater and massive AoE with Hell and Genesis. 5. **Terraquake** — replaces Crustal Crush; helps break tough enemies while generating Earth stains
6. **Wildfire** or **Ice Lance** — Ice Lance replaces Thermal Transfer in Act 3 because it generates stains, which Thermal Transfer cannot; Wildfire is the alternative if you want AoE burn application

**One complication worth flagging:** sources diverge on her endgame weapon.
