Two questions, two answers:

**Attributes after crit cap**

All weapons factor the wielder's Might into their power in addition to their two scaling attributes, meaning that putting points into Might is essentially double-dipping when it comes to boosting damage. The Attack Power gains from Might scale with the equipped weapon's base power — 99 points of Might might increase Attack Power by ~600 with a high-base-power weapon, but only ~200 with a low-base-power weapon.

Your main team weapons (Lithum, Chevalam, Litheson) are all at level 33, so they have maximum base power — Might will be at its most effective here.

The second question is whether increasing base Luck/Defence to free up Pictos crit is worth it. The answer is broadly no for your current characters — Agility and Luck are already at 99, and Maelle/Verso are already well over crit cap. More Luck doesn't help once you're capped. Defence gives Speed and Defence stat, but you're both sitting at Defence 0 with no Defence attribute investment at all.

**Concrete recommendation per character:**

- **Maelle and Verso (A99, L99, D0):** Put remaining points into Might. The weapons are high-level enough that the returns are meaningful, and there's nowhere better to put them. Defence would give some Speed and Defence stat but they're DPS, not tanks, and speed is already adequate.
- **Sciel (A99, L99, D63):** She's already splitting into Defence for Litheson scaling. Continue Defence investment if Litheson scales with it; otherwise Might.
- **Lune (V99, A99, L51):** Her Luck is notably lower than the others at 51. She's at 101% crit total, but her base crit from Luck is only 29% — more of that is coming from Pictos. If you wanted to reduce Pictos crit dependency, Lune is the one where more Luck investment would actually change something. That said, at level 86 with V99 and A99, she has no free attribute headroom — this would require a Recoat respec.
- **Monoco (A99, D99, L63):** He'll hit crit cap soon. After that, Might is the logical sink.

Short answer: Might is correct for Maelle and Verso. Don't invest more Luck — you're already past the crit cap and more Luck gives nothing. Respeccing isn't worth it for the others at this stage.

**Perfect Chroma Catalysts**

There are 10 Perfect Chroma Catalysts in the base game, spread across: Flying Manor (4 — one each from Lampmaster, Eveque trio, Dualliste, and Clea), Serpenphare on the Continent (1), Trash-can Man in Lumière (1), Painting Workshop Lampmaster (1), Endless Tower stage 11 trial 3 (1), and Chromatic Creation in Renoir's Drafts (1). Plus 2 from defeating Simon in The Abyss.

You have 2, so you've had Flying Manor's 4 plus Serpenphare. The remaining pre-Renoir's Drafts one you may be missing is the Trash-can Man in Lumière — only available if you spoke to him in the prologue. Worth checking. Everything else (Tower, Chromatic Creation, Simon) is still ahead.

Since Chromatic Creation respawns, you can farm as many Perfect Catalysts as you like once you reach Renoir's Drafts — so scarcity is only a first-playthrough concern.

On upgrading Kralim and Joyaro: I'd hold the 2 Perfects for now. Kralim and Joyaro are reserve weapons — they see limited use. The main team weapons (Lithum, Chevalam, Litheson) are already at 33, which is correct. Levelling a weapon from 32 to 33 nearly doubles its base power, so the upgrade is impactful, but reserve weapons hitting 33 is lower priority than saving Perfects for when you eventually acquire Simoso (Verso's endgame), Scaverim (Lune's best-in-slot), or Charnon (Sciel). I'd wait until you either have more Catalysts from Renoir's Drafts, or until you've confirmed those later weapons aren't coming soon.
