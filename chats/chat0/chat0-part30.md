Short answer (safe to read): direct path → story progression, fewer optional rewards; side paths → optional challenges, loot, and learning opportunities; yes, you can come back later — nothing here is permanently missable.

**What "direct vs side paths" actually means in Visages**

Visages is structured as a hub-like gauntlet, not a one-way corridor.

Direct path: advances the main narrative, fewer fights, lower cognitive load, gets you to the next story beat faster. This is the "I want to see what happens next" route.

Side paths contain tougher encounters, optional elites, and extra rewards (gear, Lumina/Pictos, upgrade materials). They tend to stress endurance more than burst and expose longer attack patterns (good for parry learning). They are explicitly optional.

**Can you come back later?**

Yes. Visages does not lock you out permanently based on path choice. You will not lose story content, access to Visages, side-path rewards, or completion opportunities. You can push forward now and come back later when you want more challenge, want to test builds, or are mopping up content.

**How to choose for your goals**

If you want momentum and narrative flow → take the direct path first. If you want low-frustration parry practice and endurance reps → explore side paths. If you're undecided → do one side path, then reassess. There is no "correct" order.
